# Social Engineering

- Social engineering attacks manipulate people into revealing sensitive information or performing unsafe actions.

----

# Phishing 
- social engineering attack
- attack vector
    - phone call
    - sms
    - email
- dangerous because even with strong security controls, attackers can gain access if a user:
    - Clicks a malicious link
    - Downloads a malicious attachment
    - Runs malicious software. Once this happens, attackers may gain a foothold inside the organization’s network.
- Role of a Security Analyst
    - When malicious emails bypass filters, analysts must:
          - Analyze the email
          - Determine if it is malicious or benign
          - Extract indicators
          - Update security systems to block similar emails

# Types of Phishing Attacks

## 1. Spam
- Bulk unsolicited emails sent to many recipients.
- Malicious spam is known as: Malspam

## 2. Phishing
- Emails pretending to be legitimate organizations to steal sensitive information.
- Targets:
    - Credentials
    - Banking details
    - Personal information

## 3. Spear Phishing
- More targeted version of phishing.
- Characteristics:
    - Specific individual or organization targeted
    - Highly personalized messages

## 4. Whaling
- Targets high-level executives such as:
    - CEO
    - CFO
    - Executives

## 5. Smishing
- Phishing through SMS/text messages.

## 6. Vishing
- Phishing through voice calls.
